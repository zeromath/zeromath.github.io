Etale Cohomology(1)

Posted on 2017-08-16 | In Math |

This is a note of a summer mini course in University of Michigan

Sites

Definition
A presheaf on a category $\mathcal C$ is a contravariant functor $\mathcal C^\mathrm{op}\rightarrow \mathrm{Set}$

Let $\hat{\mathcal C}$ be the category of presheaves on $\mathcal C$. Then we have following facts:

  1. $\hat{\mathcal C}$ is complete: it has all Limits and Colimits
  2. (Yoneda Embedding) For each $X\in \mathcal C$, let $h_X$ be the functor such that $h_X(Y)=\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal C}(Y,X)$, then $h$ induces an embedding $\mathcal C\rightarrow \hat{\mathcal C}$

Next we want to find out what is a sheaf on a category. For this purpose, we need to understand what is an open cover on a category $\mathcal C$. Therefore we introduce Grothendieck Topology.

Definition
Let $\mathcal C$ be a category with pullbacks. A Grothendieck Topology on $\mathcal C$ is an assignment
$$
U\mapsto \mathrm{Cov}(U)\subseteq \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal C,\downarrow,U)
$$where $\mathrm{Cov}(U)$ is a collection of objects over $U$ such that
1) The isomorphism $U\rightarrow U$ is a cover.
2) If $(U_i\rightarrow U|i\in I)$ is a cover of $U$ and $V\rightarrow U$, then $(V\times_U U_i\rightarrow V|i\in I)$ is a cover of $V$.
3) If $(U_i\rightarrow U|i\in I)$ is a cover of $U$ and $(U_{ij}\rightarrow U_i|j\in I_j)$ is a cover of $U_i$, then $(U_{ij}\rightarrow U|i,j)$ is a cover of $U$.


Exercise If $(U_i\rightarrow X|i\in I)$ and $(V_j\rightarrow X|j\in J)$ are two covers of $X$, then $(U_i\times_X V_j \rightarrow X|i\in I,j\in J)$ is a cover of $X$.
Answer
Since $(U_i\rightarrow X|i\in I)$ is a cover of $X$ and we have the identity map $X\rightarrow X$, by Axiom 2 we see that $(U_i\times_X X\rightarrow X|i\in I)$ is a cover of $X$. Now for each $U_i\times_X X$, $(U_i\times_X V_j\rightarrow U_i\times_X X|j\in J)$ is a cover(this is again by axiom 2), so the conclusion follows.
Definition
A site is a category together with a Grothendieck Topology

Examples

  1. $\mathcal C=\mathrm{Open}(X)$ while $\mathrm{Cov}(U)=(U_i\rightarrow U)$ where $\cup_i U_i = U$.
  2. $X$ is an affine scheme, $\mathcal C=X_\mathrm{aff}$ the collection of all distinguished open susbets of $X$. The topology is the same as (1).
  3. $\mathcal C=G$-sets (sets with a group $G$ action), the open cover is $(X_i\rightarrow^{\phi_i} X)$ if $\cup_i\phi_i(X_i)=X$.
  4. $\mathcal C=\mathrm{Top}$, the open cover is $(X_i\rightarrow^{\phi_i} X)$ if $\cup_i\phi_i(X_i)=X$ where $\phi_i$ is a local homeomorphism (etale)
  5. $X$ is a topological space, $\mathcal C=X_{\mathrm{et}}$ (etale spaces over $X$)
  6. $\mathcal C=(\mathrm{spec}(k))_\mathrm{et}$ where objects are $\prod_\mathrm{finite} \mathrm{spec}(L)\rightarrow \mathrm{spec}(k)$ where $L$ are finite seperable extensions of $k$. (jointly surjective topology)

Sheaves

Definition
Let $\mathcal C$ be a site, a presheaf $F\in\hat{\mathcal C}$ is a sheaf if for any $U\in \mathcal C$, any cover $(U_i\rightarrow U|i\in I)$ and any element $s_i\in F(U_i)|i\in I$ such that
$$
F(U_i\times_U U_j\rightarrow U_i)(s_i)=F(U_i\times_U U_j\rightarrow U_j)(s_j)
$$
then there is a unique $s\in F(U)$ such that $F(U_i\rightarrow U)(s)=s_i$.
Or in other words, we have an equalizer
$$
F(U)\rightarrow F(U_i)\Rightarrow F(U_i\times_U U_j)
$$

We shall use following notations:

  • $\mathrm{PSh}(\mathcal C)$: presheaves of Sets
  • $\mathrm{PAb}(\mathcal C)$: presheaves of Abelian Groups
  • $\mathrm{Sh}(\mathcal C)$: Sheaves of Sets
  • $\mathrm{Ab}(\mathcal C)$: Sheaves of Abelian Groups

Exercise

  1. Let $X$ be an affine scheme, then the map $\mathrm{Open}(X)\rightarrow X_\mathrm{aff}$ induces equivalence $\mathrm{Sh}(\mathrm{Open}(X))\rightarrow \mathrm{Sh}(X_\mathrm{aff})$
  2. Let $X$ be an topological space, then the map $\mathrm{Open}(X)\rightarrow X_\mathrm{et}$ induces equivalence $\mathrm{Sh}(\mathrm{Open}(X))\rightarrow \mathrm{Sh}(X_\mathrm{et})$
Definition
$\mathcal C$ is a Grothendieck topos if $\mathcal C$ is equivalent to $\mathrm{Sh}(\mathcal D)$ for some site $\mathcal D$.

Now we look at $\mathrm{spec}(k)_\mathrm{et}$

$L\supseteq k$ is a finite Galois extension, then $G_{L/k}=\mathrm{Aut}_{\mathrm{spec}(k)}(\mathrm{spec}(L))$

For any $F\in\mathrm{Sh}(\mathrm{spec}(k))_\mathrm{et})$, $F(\mathrm{spec}(L))$ is a $G_{L/k}$-set

Exercise $L\otimes_k L\rightarrow \prod_{\sigma\in \mathrm{Gal}(L/k)} L$ which sending $x\otimes y$ to $(x\sigma y)_\sigma$ is an isomorphism.

We have an equalizer:
$$
F(\mathrm{spec}(k))\rightarrow F(\mathrm{spec}(L))\Rightarrow F(\mathrm{spec}(L\otimes_k L))
$$

Conclusion: $F(\mathrm{spec}(k))=F(\mathrm{spec}(k))^{\mathrm{G}_{L/k}}$

Fact Sheaf cohomology on $\mathrm{spec}(k)_\mathrm{et}$ is Galois cohomology.
